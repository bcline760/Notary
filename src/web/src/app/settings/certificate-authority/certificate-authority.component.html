Common Names

This section will give the root and signing (intermediate) a name which would appear in the certificate path for your issued certificates. Example of a common name would be "My Company Signing RSA SHA256 CA" or "My Company Root CA". Both are required.

Issuer Details

The fields below give information about the Certificate Authority. Which organization (company, government, etc), the org unit (like IT), and where it is located. These are all optional but, to give additional credence to your CA, fill out as much and as accurately as possible.

Key Length

Select one of: 1,024, 2,048, or 4,096 as one of your key lengths. Industry standard is 2,048. 4,096 is stronger, but it will require more computational effort using the certificate to sign, encrypt, etc.

CA Validity Duration

Pick one of 5, 10, 15 years. Typical length is 10 years for a CA certificate. Too short of a time and you will have to renew your CA frequently which might cause difficulty with your issued certificate. Too long of a time increases CA compromise and obsolecense.
